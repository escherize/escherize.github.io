* Interview TIPS
** Show appreciation for them considering you for the role.
*** Thanks for spending time to get me the more competitive offer
** Show enthusiasm to work with the team and how you'd like to contribute to the team's success
   With some skills you have that will help them, e.g: Startup Acumen, Technical Abilities, Mentorship Experience [Helps Growth]
** Think about it as working _together_ with the hiring manager to find a package that fits both parties
*** Create a win-win scenario
** Ask for the company's salary, and total compensation packages as well as growth possibilities.
** Admit to being uncomfortable stating a fixed number without knowing salary possibilities.
** Worst case, let them get back to you and provide you with a =range of how much the company values nthe position=
** say: 'It's my Dream Job and its something that I am working toward with you'
** Be comfortable with asking the company's range of benefits
** They won't disregard your application if you're just asking for how much they value the position you're filling in
** After Salary is dialed in, explore other benefits (vacation, stock options, shorter review cycle, WFH stipend, etc.)
* Research the Job
** get salary info from someone you know at the company, levels.fyi, or glassdoor
** show why your experience is directly relevant to the company's values
*** use their nomenclature - this is very persuasive!!
    Go into their job description and re-use the same vocabulary. This can make you seem like you fit in.
    "Well I'm currently a 'Senior Fullstack Developer', and ..." etc.

* Scripted Answers to questions:
**  Current Salary?                                          :current:salary:
   - I'm not comfortable sharing that information I would prefer to
     focus on the value I can add to this company and not what I'm
     paid at my current job.
   - At my current role I am very well compensated, and I'm certainly
     looking for compensation that is a good fit for me, but I'm also
     looking for a better fit in terms of my values and where I want
     to be with my career.
** Desired Salary?                                           :desired:salary:
   - I want this move to be a big step forward for me in terms of both
     responsibility and compensation.
   - I would rather know more about the job before I discuss that, why dont you tell me more about the salary possibilities at this job?
   - Its harder for me to name a number without knowing about the
     role, total compensation package, and growth possibilities - so
     can u help me by explaining what =Company= has to say about that?
   - I dont feel comfortable naming a number without more information,
     so if you can get me some info I can get back to you about that.
   - I'm interested in the full compensation package + I'm interested
     in career growth opportunities.
   - I'd love to hear what you're willing to offer and what =Company=
     has to say about it, and we can move forward from there, I'd like
     to contiune the conversation after you make an offer.
** How much is your other offer for?                                  :offer:
   - It's a competitive offer, but I'm interested in the whole package: the work I do, the value I can provide, and all the different forms of compensation, and how that can fit with =company=
** Why do you want to work here?
*** highlight why the company is cool and interesting
*** interesting problems / challenges for the company
    Could be data ingestion / staffing issues / etc.
** If you recieved a =Low Offer=                                  :low:offer:
   - I'm excited to get working here + appreciate the offer
   - In terms of the salary for the responsibilites / etc. this number is quite far off. I want this job and i want to work here, but right now we are pretty far off.
*** Instead of giving in to a low number
    - Could you please walk me through the numbers?
    - How did you reach that salary?
*** "This is the standard rate we pay everyone"
    - I understand this is a standard rate for a typical candidate, but I'm not a typical candidate based on my experience and based on the skill sets that I'm bringing to the job.

****** here's how to do it right:

**** Minimum accepable salary (set this b4 negotiating)
***** reference points
****** What are other people making?
****** Pending raises and bonuses
****** Overall feel
**** When you get an offer, counter between 10-20% above their offer.
***** 10% is a given / bare minimum
***** 20% is more pushing it, it's a good spot if you dont really need the job
****** they really need you to do the job + U can take it or leave it.
**** Force them to define the negotiation window
***** never ever ever disclose ur current or desired salary
****** This negotiation is now anchored around _their_ offer

****** initial offer 50k
****** counter @ 56k (12% above - close to the 10% floor.)
